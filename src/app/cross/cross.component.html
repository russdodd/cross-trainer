Cross To F2L

A tool to practice cross and transition to F2L

How tracking difficulty works {{ showMethodology ? '▴' : '▾' }}

Only pairs whose corner ends on top count. Once the cross is solved a corner is either on top or buried in a slot, and buried means digging it out first — so those pairs are excluded, however easy they were to follow.

Difficulty is disruptions: how many moves of the cross solution displace the pair's corner or edge, counted even if a later move puts the piece back.

Each scramble is graded by its best qualifying pair — the one you'd actually pick.

  • Easy 0–2 disruptions
  • Medium 3–4
  • Hard 5+

Levels 1–2 have no hard cases — a short cross can't disrupt a pair five times. Reveal the solution to see every pair's counts and where each piece ended up.
